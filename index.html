The Story

A Heartwarming Lost And Found Children's Story Based On A True Childhood Memory In 1980s England

Baxter Is Missing! is a beautifully illustrated children's picture book for ages
3 to 6. Perfect as a bedtime story or classroom read-aloud, it helps young children explore feelings of loss, hope, and reassurance in a gentle, age-appropriate way.

In the big, busy town of Basingstoke, young Paul and his teddy bear, Baxter, are the best of friends. They go everywhere together: to the park, to the bakery, even on an airplane!. But one day, after a ride on the big red bus, Paul makes a heartbreaking discovery. Baxter has been left behind.

Inspired by a true story and set against the backdrop of 1980s England, "Baxter Is Missing!" tells the tender journey of a boy searching for his teddy and the teddy who never stops hoping to be found. As Baxter tumbles through the streets, the market, and even onto a theatre stage, kindness from strangers helps guide him back home.

About The Author

Paul Mannion grew up in the town of Basingstoke, England during the 1980s and 1990s, always clutching his beloved teddy, Mr. Ted (Baxter), by his side. One fateful morning on a crowded bus into the town centre, Paul’s Mum realised he’d been left behind.

For three long months she phoned the bus station, hoping someone would call to say he had turned up. Finally, a friendly bus-station employee rang with good news: Mr. Ted had been found! He was a little worse for wear, a missing eye and nose, but Paul’s Mum carefully stitched him a bright new nose, and he was as good as new.

The story is made up of personal stories, memories and embodies real people in Paul's life. Some of the places and people mentioned in the story are sadly no longer with us, but this story is to help remember those people and places.

Paul lives in Dublin, Ireland with his wife, their dog, and of course, the one-eyed Mr. Ted still by his side for every twist and turn life has to offer.
